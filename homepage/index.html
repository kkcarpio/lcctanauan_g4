Historical Timeline of La Consolacion College Tanauan

LA CONSOLACION COLLEGE UNIFICATION

2015: Regional Integration of ASAS schools in a Unified System. Sr. Emilia Lacuarta, OSA, decided to integrate LCCT and LCCB, forming the Unified La Consolacion College – South Luzon (ULCC-SL).

INNOVATIONS & REMARKABLE ACHIEVEMENTS

SY 2012-2013: K-12 IMPLEMENTATION. Ms. Remedios Molinyawe, along with other academic administrators, implemented the K-12 program of DepEd. Foreign languages like Spanish and Nihongo were added to the curriculum.

SY 2012-2013: PAASCU LEVEL II STATUS. The Grade School and High School Department were granted Level II re-accreditation, ensuring five more years of Level II status.

UbD Implementation

SY 2010-2011: Ms. Remedios Molinyawe and Mrs. Erlinda Padilla initiated the implementation of "Understanding by Design" (UbD).

Emerald Jubilee

2008: LCCT celebrated its Sapphire Jubilee, with Sr. Emilia Lacuarta, OSA, focusing on innovative and transformative education.

College Department First Graduation Rites

March 31, 2007: LCCT held its first college graduation, celebrating 13 graduates from various programs such as BSA, BSED, BEED, and BSBA.

PAASCU VISIT

2003: LCCT passed the PAASCU accreditation for both the High School and Grade School Departments during formal surveys conducted in September 2003.

BUILDING CONSTRUCTION

2002-2003: Construction of a four-story building housing High School and College Departments began after a groundbreaking ceremony on August 18, 2002. CHED approved new tertiary programs this year.

Golden Jubilee

1998-1999: OLFA celebrated its Golden Jubilee with a week-long celebration.

Continual Improvement for Stability and Growth

1988-1994: Sr. Concepcion Leopoldo, OSA, named the new buildings "St. Augustine Building." A multi-purpose hall and a gymnasium were constructed with alumni support.

Revolutionizing the Catholic-Augustinian Education

1978-1985: Sr. Ma. Fidelis encouraged adopting the Catholic School System Development (CS-SD) program for quality assurance and accreditation.

Revitalizing Students’ Potential

1970-1978: Sisters Susana Agudelo and Adelina Segismundo re-engineered school activities for holistic student development. The OLFA Alumni Association launched in 1973.

Transforming the Barung-Barung School into OLFA

1948-1970: OLFA's school building opened on October 24, 1948, serving 35 pioneer students. The school became one of the region's best institutions.

The Church and Augustinian Education

1948: Augustinian Friars established Catholic schools in the Philippines, including OLFA in Tanauan, Batangas.

Our Lady of Fatima (OLFA) Established

June 20, 1948: Sor. Encarnacion de los Remedios, OSA, with three nuns, organized and led OLFA, starting its educational mission in Tanauan.
